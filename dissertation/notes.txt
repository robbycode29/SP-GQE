The (n, tau) pair (n - nth degree of graph node traversal, tau - graph node pruning factor)
can be self regulating. (to be explored).

The decision whether to further expand the search to the connections of a node or to remove an already
added node by pruning in the Graph Expansion phase,
can be taken individually per node based on tau, thus creating controlled expansion, a situation where relevant
information is expanded upon and irrelevant information is pruned at node level, iteratively (compared to the initial
approach which loaded a fixed set of node relations e.g. i=10, n=3 --> 1000 nodes and then pruned them all at once)
thus allowing for a more focused trial and error search (like that of fungi or tree roots in search of nutrients or
water)
